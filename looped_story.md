# LOOPED — Game Concept & Narrative Fusion (Beta Framework)

## 1. The Protagonist Shift: The 80-Year Loop
You are not a conscripted soldier or a reluctant mage; you are a wanderer who simply walked too far out of safety. You stumbled upon a **Time Sphere**—a glowing, anomalous relic of the Void. Touching it didn't just pull you in; it trapped you in a closed temporal loop for the last 80 years. 

This Time Sphere is now your permanent anchor. It is the literal Gate that teleports you into the dungeon for each run, and the tether that yanks you back when you die. You aren't fighting to save the world; you are fighting to finally break an 8-decade cycle. This temporal trap creates a deeply satisfying, logical world-building progression where the rules of the isolated world are a physical puzzle you must master through repetition.

## 2. Realigning "Erasure": The Arm Monitor as Containment
To reward the player for chasing god-tier builds, the concept of "Alignment" is preserved but reframed entirely around your hardware. 

The 7 Void Frequencies still want to consume reality. However, your **Arm Monitor** acts as a localized containment cell. Slotting passive cards doesn't corrupt your soul; it weaponizes a controlled leak of that cosmic frequency. Pushing your build to the absolute limit is the power fantasy of forcing the Void to work for you. "The Final Convergence" is simply what happens when the hardware fails. The final boss, "The Looped," is a reflection of your possible fate: a Hunter whose monitor shattered long ago, leaving him permanently merged with his own broken combo.

## 3. The Found Family: Single-Rescue Extraction
Instead of starting with a populated hub, you start completely alone with the Time Sphere. Lysa, Brann, Serin, and Mira are lost somewhere within the dungeon. 

During your runs, you will encounter them and must complete a one-time rescue event. Once extracted, they return to the hub area around the Time Sphere permanently. Each rescued character will grant you a specific, persistent buff to aid in future loops. Their presence mechanically transforms the hub from a lonely prison into a growing base camp without adding premature scope to the beta.

## 4. Pacing the Madness: Beta-Friendly Escalation
Since the game is currently in the beta phase, the development scope is kept highly manageable. Advanced, engine-breaking dynamic audio or shifting geometry are set aside for later stages. 

Instead, the "descent into madness" is handled purely through standard, achievable roguelike escalation:
* **Floor 1:** Standard lighting, clean enemy behaviors, and basic combat geometry.
* **Floor 2 & 3:** The environmental storytelling shifts using static assets and simpler variables. The lighting palette changes (e.g., turning sickly or high-contrast), enemy density spikes, and the architecture of the generated rooms utilizes more chaotic, pre-built prefabs.
* **The Reset:** The psychological weight comes entirely from the loop itself. Whether you clear the floor or get crushed, the Time Sphere violently resets you back to zero.
